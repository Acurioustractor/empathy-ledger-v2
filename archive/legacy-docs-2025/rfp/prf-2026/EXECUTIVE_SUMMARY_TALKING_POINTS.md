## Potential Challenges & How We'll Address Them

### Challenge: "Is this just building better software?"
**Response:** No‚Äîit's generating evidence for policy change, redirecting philanthropic investment, and shifting how technology sector engages with Indigenous communities. Software is the vehicle, systems change is the destination.

### Challenge: "How is this different from other Indigenous digital projects?"
**Response:** Three key differences: (1) OCAP¬Æ embedded in technical architecture from day one, not retrofitted; (2) Proven community outcomes, not theoretical pilots; (3) Focus on systems change and replicable models, not one-off solutions.

### Challenge: "Can this really scale?"
**Response:** Platform already supports 226 users across multiple projects. Multi-tenant architecture designed for unlimited communities. Open-source foundation enables community self-hosting and adaptation. We're not scaling one platform‚Äîwe're scaling a model.

### Challenge: "What if communities don't want digital platforms?"
**Response:** Participatory research ensures community voice and choice. We document what works and for whom, not forcing one-size-fits-all solutions. Some communities may choose different approaches‚Äîthat's self-determination in action.

### Challenge: "How do you balance research rigor with community benefit?"
**Response:** Participatory action research where communities are co-researchers. Indigenous Advisory Board has veto power over research outputs. Community benefit is primary, academic publication is secondary.

### Challenge: "What happens after fellowship ends?"
**Response:** Open-source platform continues under community ownership. Policy changes create lasting impact. Published frameworks enable others to replicate. Community capacity built during fellowship enables ongoing leadership.

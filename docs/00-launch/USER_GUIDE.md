## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. **Visit the Platform**
   - Go to your organization's Empathy Ledger URL
   - Click "Sign Up" or use the invitation link sent by your organization

2. **Complete Your Profile**
   - Add your name and cultural affiliations
   - Set your privacy preferences
   - Configure cultural protocols (if applicable)
   - Review and accept OCAP principles

3. **Verify Your Account**
   - Check your email for verification link
   - Click to verify and activate your account

### First Login

After logging in for the first time:
- Take the platform tour (optional)
- Review your privacy settings
- Explore the dashboard
- Create your first story (when ready)

## Creating Stories

### Story Types

1. **Personal Stories**
   - Individual experiences
   - Life events
   - Cultural knowledge

2. **Project Stories**
   - Part of an organization project
   - Collaborative storytelling
   - Community initiatives

3. **Sacred Stories**
   - Require Elder review
   - Protected access

### Creating a New Story

1. **Start a Story**
   - Click "Create Story" from your dashboard
   - Choose story type
   - Select project (if applicable)

2. **Add Content**
   - Write or upload your story
   - Add a title and description
   - Include cultural context
   - Tag themes and topics

3. **Add Media**
   - Upload photos, videos, or audio
   - Add captions and descriptions
   - Set media privacy levels
   - Include cultural protocols

4. **Set Privacy & Consent**
   - Choose who can view your story
   - Set consent duration
   - Add trigger warnings (if needed)
   - Specify sharing permissions

5. **Submit for Review**
   - Review your story
   - Submit to Elder (if required)
   - Await approval
   - Publish when ready

### Editing Stories

- **Draft Mode**: Edit freely before publishing
- **Published Mode**: Request edits through your organization
- **Version History**: View previous versions
- **Collaboration**: Work with others on shared stories

## Cultural Safety

### OCAP Principles

**Ownership, Control, Access, Possession**

The Empathy Ledger is built on OCAP principles:
- **Ownership**: You own your stories
- **Control**: You control access and use
- **Access**: You decide who can view
- **Possession**: You can download and delete your data

### Sacred Content Protection

**What is Sacred Content?**
- Ceremonial knowledge
- Restricted stories
- Elder-only information
- Seasonal or gender-specific content

**Protection Measures:**
- Elder review required
- Additional consent checks
- Access restrictions
- No AI analysis (unless approved)
- Special sharing protocols

### Consent Management

**Ongoing Consent:**
- Review consent every 6-12 months
- Withdraw consent anytime
- Update sharing preferences
- Revoke specific permissions

**Types of Consent:**
- Story publication
- Media usage
- AI analysis (opt-in)
- Data sharing with partners
- Research participation

### Common Questions

**Q: How do I change my privacy settings?**
A: Go to Dashboard â†’ Settings â†’ Privacy. You can set global defaults and per-story settings.

**Q: Can I delete a published story?**
A: Yes, go to the story page and click "Delete". Note: Some organizations may archive stories rather than delete them.

**Q: How do I withdraw consent?**
A: Go to Dashboard â†’ Consent Management, select the story, and click "Withdraw Consent".

**Q: Who can see my stories?**
A: Only people you've granted access to based on your privacy settings. You can check who has viewed your story in the analytics.

**Q: How do I report inappropriate content?**
A: Click the "Report" button on any story or contact your organization admin.

**Q: Can I export all my stories?**
A: Yes, go to Settings â†’ Export Data to download all your stories and media.

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Can't Upload Media**
- Check file size limits
- Verify file format is supported
- Ensure stable internet connection
- Try a different browser

**Story Not Publishing**
- Check if Elder review is required
- Verify all required fields are completed
- Ensure consent is current
- Contact support if issue persists

**Privacy Settings Not Saving**
- Clear browser cache
- Try a different browser
- Disable browser extensions
- Contact support

**Can't Access Story**
- Verify you have permission
- Check if consent has expired
- Ensure you're logged in
- Contact the storyteller or organization

## Best Practices

### For Storytellers

1. **Be Authentic**: Share your genuine experiences
2. **Respect Protocols**: Follow cultural guidelines
3. **Seek Consent**: Get permission when featuring others
4. **Protect Sacred Knowledge**: Use Elder review when appropriate
5. **Engage Thoughtfully**: Respond to comments with respect
6. **Review Regularly**: Update your stories and consent

### For Organizations

1. **Clear Guidelines**: Provide storytellers with clear cultural protocols
2. **Support Elders**: Ensure Elders have time and resources for review
3. **Monitor Analytics**: Track engagement and impact
4. **Celebrate Stories**: Recognize and amplify important stories
5. **Cultural Safety First**: Prioritize cultural appropriateness over quantity
6. **Regular Review**: Check consent status and privacy settings

### For Elders

1. **Take Your Time**: Thorough review is more important than speed
2. **Provide Guidance**: Help storytellers understand cultural protocols
3. **Protect Sacred Knowledge**: Don't hesitate to restrict access when needed
4. **Support Storytellers**: Encourage and guide, not just approve/reject
5. **Document Protocols**: Share cultural guidelines for the community
6. **Stay Engaged**: Participate in the platform's cultural governance

## Glossary

**ALMA**: Autonomy, Livelihood, Meaning, Awareness - a framework for ethical storytelling

**OCAP**: Ownership, Control, Access, Possession - Indigenous data sovereignty principles

**RLS (Row Level Security)**: Database-level security that enforces access controls

**Sacred Content**: Cultural knowledge that requires special protocols and restricted access

**SROI**: Social Return on Investment - a methodology for measuring cultural and social value

**Storyteller**: A platform user who shares their stories

**Elder**: A designated cultural authority who reviews stories for cultural appropriateness

**Organization**: A tenant on the multi-tenant platform (e.g., Indigenous community, NGO)

**Consent**: Ongoing permission to share and use stories

**Cultural Protocol**: Guidelines for respectful engagement with cultural content

## Platform Values

The Empathy Ledger is built on these core values:

1. **Cultural Sovereignty**: Communities control their own stories
2. **Ongoing Consent**: Permission is never permanent
3. **Sacred Content Protection**: Some knowledge requires special care
4. **Intergenerational Respect**: Honor Elders and traditional knowledge
5. **Transparency**: Clear about data use and access
6. **Dignity**: Every story and storyteller is treated with respect
7. **Amplification, Not Exploitation**: Platform serves storytellers, not advertisers
